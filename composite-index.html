Composite is a 1–100-scaled weighted geometric mean; Cov. is how many indicators the score is built on (amber = a core indicator is n/a and was not imputed). Indicator columns are the 1–100 normalized values. Hover an indicator header for its definition.

Weighting scheme (fixed & transparent)

IndicatorWeightDefinition
Public-outlay effort (% GDP)40%sum(public_outlay_usd) over appropriated/outlay actors, deduplicated by event_key, divided by GDP x 100. The only cardinal-rule-compliant aggregate; headline figures are never summed. A genuine measured 0 (no appropriated outlay) is kept, not treated as missing.
Fiscal prioritization (x GBARD)20%public outlay / annual government budget for R&D (GBARD). n/a (NOT imputed) where GBARD is not confidently known — China, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Singapore, Australia, Israel.
Program breadth (domains)15%count of distinct domains (ai / quantum / semiconductor / compute / ai+quantum) the jurisdiction has at least one record in (1-5). A portfolio-diversity signal.
Evidence quality (0-1)25%verification-weighted mean across the jurisdiction's records (verified 1.0, reported 0.6, estimate 0.3, unconfirmed 0.1). Rewards well-sourced commitments over unverified mobilization headlines.
Normalization. min-max per indicator across ranked jurisdictions, rescaled to [1,100] (lower bound 1, not 0, so geometric aggregation is well-defined).
Aggregation. weighted GEOMETRIC mean over AVAILABLE indicators only. Missing indicators are n/a and are NEVER imputed; the unit's weights are renormalized over what it has, and its indicator coverage (k of N) is reported alongside the score.
Uncertainty. Monte Carlo: each draw jitters (a) every indicator's underlying value by a per-jurisdiction data-confidence sigma and (b) the weights by +-25% (renormalized), then re-normalizes and re-ranks. 90% rank confidence intervals are the 5th-95th percentile of each jurisdiction's rank across draws.
By design: The index rewards genuine appropriated PUBLIC OUTLAY, not announcement headlines (the cardinal rule). Jurisdictions whose commitments are sovereign-wealth / private mobilization / state-fund capital (e.g. Saudi Arabia, UAE, much of China and France's headline) therefore score low on the outlay indicators by design — their figures are not appropriated public spend. This is a feature, not a bug.
